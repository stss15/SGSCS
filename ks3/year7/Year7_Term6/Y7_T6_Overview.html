Data Representation

Unit Summary

Ever wonder how computers understand everything from numbers to music and pictures? This topic explores the fascinating ways computers represent all kinds of information using their own special codes. You'll focus on the most important one, binary – the computer's core language made of just 0s and 1s! You'll also see how this connects to the denary numbers you use every day and another helpful system called hexadecimal. You will get the big picture of how computers cleverly translate text, sound, and images into a digital format they can understand and work with.
